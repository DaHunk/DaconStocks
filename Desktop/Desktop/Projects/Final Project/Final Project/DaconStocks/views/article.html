Investing in the stock market

Quick answer: The basic idea behind investing in the stock market is to put money you’ve saved into stocks you think will increase in value over time. There is no guarantee that you will make money in stocks. Making your investments pay off takes a lot of work. You need to follow the financial news, use the market indices such as the Dow Jones Industrial Average , S&P 500, and the NASDAQ Composite to watch market trends, and thoroughly research companies you want to invest in.

What Is the Stock Market and How Does It Work?

The term “stock market” often refers to one of the major stock market indexes, such as the Dow Jones Industrial Average or the S&P 500. Because it’s hard to track every single stock, these indexes include a section of the stock market and their performance is viewed as representative of the entire market.
